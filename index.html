Background and Motivation

On near-term hardware, the binding cost of the quantum approximate optimization algorithm is the number of objective evaluations spent locating good variational angles, not circuit depth.

The quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA) is a leading candidate for near-term quantum advantage on combinatorial problems and a standard testbed for variational algorithms in the noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) era. On the canonical MaxCut problem — NP-hard, with the Goemans–Williamson classical guarantee — a depth-$p$ circuit alternates a cost-Hamiltonian phase with a transverse-field mixer under $2p$ angles $(\bm{\gamma},\bm{\beta})$ and maximizes the expected cut $\langle C(\bm{\gamma},\bm{\beta})\rangle$. Each evaluation of $\langle C\rangle$ on hardware consumes many circuit executions and shots, and the variational optimizer queries the objective repeatedly; angle optimization is therefore the rate-limiting step, compounded by barren plateaus and cost concentration in high-dimensional landscapes. The operative figure of merit is consequently the cut a policy reaches within a fixed query budget, alongside the best attainable cut.

A warm start targets precisely this cost: supplying good initial angles places the optimizer inside a high-quality basin from the outset. Three empirical regularities lend warm starts their force — the QAOA objective concentrates across instances that share structure, optimal parameters transfer between random graphs and across sizes, and purpose-built initializers (annealing schedules, relaxation rounding, classical surrogates, meta-learning) reliably seed the optimizer well. Depth one is the cleanest setting in which to study them: the expected cut admits an exact closed form, so the dependence of good angles on local graph structure is open to analytic inspection at negligible cost.

The open problem. A sharp learning question follows. A policy that conditions on graph topology — ideally through a permutation-invariant representation, in the spirit of graph neural networks and Weisfeiler–Lehman kernels — should propose warm-start angles that transfer to unseen graph families and improve on a simpler initializer. The literature offers many such proposals but few controlled tests; leakage-aware, query-counted, family-held-out comparisons against a strong-but-simple spectral baseline remain scarce. Absent those controls, a learned component is readily credited for gains that a one-line spectral rule already supplies.

Mathematical Preliminaries

Four objects underpin every claim that follows: the MaxCut cost operator and mixer, the depth-one state, the per-edge closed-form estimator, and the relabeling-invariant descriptor generator.

Graph and spectral objects

$G=(V,E)$ is a finite simple undirected graph on $n=|V|$ vertices, adjacency $A\in\{0,1\}^{n\times n}$ (symmetric, zero diagonal), degree matrix $D=\operatorname{diag}(d_1,\dots,d_n)$ with $d_v=\sum_u A_{uv}$, and combinatorial Laplacian $L=D-A$.

A relabeling is a permutation $\pi\in S_n$ with permutation matrix $\Pi$; the relabeled graph has adjacency $A(\pi G)=\Pi A\Pi^\top$. We write $\lambda_2$ for the algebraic connectivity (second-smallest eigenvalue of $L$).

Cost Hamiltonian, mixer, and state

For MaxCut the cost operator is $C=\sum_{(u,v)\in E}\tfrac12(1-Z_uZ_v)$ and the mixer is $B=\sum_v X_v$. The depth-one state is

$|\gamma,\beta\rangle=e^{-\mathrm{i}\beta B}\,e^{-\mathrm{i}\gamma C}\,|+\rangle^{\otimes n}.$

The objective is the expected cut $\langle C(\gamma,\beta)\rangle=\langle\gamma,\beta|\,C\,|\gamma,\beta\rangle$, maximized over $(\gamma,\beta)$ at $p=1$.

Depth-one per-edge estimator

Fix an edge $(u,v)\in E$, and let $d_u,d_v$ count the neighbours of $u$ and $v$ other than the partner, with $f$ the number of common neighbours. The expected contribution of this edge to the cut has the closed form (Wang et al.):

$$\langle C_{uv}\rangle=\tfrac12+\tfrac14\sin(4\beta)\sin\gamma\,\bigl[\cos^{d_u}\!\gamma+\cos^{d_v}\!\gamma\bigr]-\tfrac14\sin^2(2\beta)\,\cos^{\,d_u+d_v-2f}\!\gamma\,\bigl[1-\cos^{f}(2\gamma)\bigr],$$

By linearity the total expected cut is $\langle C\rangle=\sum_{(u,v)\in E}\langle C_{uv}\rangle$. This $O(|E|)$ analytic estimator computes precisely the same quantity as the exact $O(p\,n\,2^n)$ statevector expectation — they agree to $10^{-9}$ — so cut quality is reported as the approximation ratio $\langle C\rangle/C^\star$ relative to the true MaxCut $C^\star$, obtained by exact enumeration of the $2^{n-1}$ distinct bipartitions.

Relabeling-invariant descriptor generator

The graph-conditioned policy sees the graph only through a fixed-length descriptor $\phi(G)\in\mathbb{R}^m$, the concatenation of six blocks of graph invariants: degree statistics (mean, std, max, min, density); motif densities (triangle density, average clustering, a 4-cycle density from $\operatorname{tr}(A^4)$); a hashed two-round, eight-bin Weisfeiler–Lehman colour histogram; cycle features (fraction of nodes in a triangle, inverse-girth proxy); Laplacian features ($\lambda_2$, spectral gap, mean and variance of the normalized-Laplacian spectrum); and connectivity features (degree assortativity, global clustering). Each component is a multiset, spectral, or trace quantity and therefore order-blind, so $\phi(\pi G)=\phi(G)$ for all $\pi\in S_n$ (proven below, verified to $10^{-8}$).

Problem Formulation

The warm-start transfer question, stated precisely: which policy reaches the highest held-out approximation ratio under a matched query budget with leakage controlled.

Fix depth $p=1$ and a per-graph query budget $B$. A warm-start policy maps a graph to initial angles $(\gamma_0,\beta_0)$ that seed a coordinate-ascent refiner; the refiner counts every call to the objective $\langle C\rangle$ and halts once the budget is spent. The hardware-relevant figure of merit is the query-budget frontier: the running-best approximation ratio as a function of the number of evaluations.

The question is then crisp. Given a corpus of connected graphs grouped into families and split into family-held-out train/test folds under a programmatic no-leakage guarantee, the test is whether a learned, topology-conditioned policy reaches a strictly higher held-out approximation ratio at matched budget $B$ than a one-line spectral initializer, a non-learned topology heuristic, and uninformed random initialization. Five policies are placed head to head:

  • Random — $\gamma_0\sim\mathcal{U}(0,\pi)$, $\beta_0\sim\mathcal{U}(0,\pi/2)$.
  • Spectral — $\gamma_0=\operatorname{clip}\!\bigl(\pi/[2(1+\lambda_2)],0,\pi\bigr)$ from the algebraic connectivity, $\beta_0=\pi/8$.
  • Topology — $\gamma_0=\arctan(1/\sqrt{\max(1,\bar d)})$ from the mean degree $\bar d$, $\beta_0=\pi/8$ (non-learned).
  • Graph-conditioned — the learned policy: a $k$-NN regressor ($k{=}3$) over standardized relabeling-invariant descriptors, fit on training families only.
  • RL (CEM) — a cross-entropy-method refiner (population 8, elite 3, 4 iterations) seeded by the graph-conditioned proposal.

Principal Contributions

The contribution is methodological: a verified evaluation framework and a controlled, fully reported finding, advanced in place of a claim of superiority.

  1. A provably relabeling-invariant graph descriptor.A fixed-length representation assembled entirely from graph invariants — degree, motif, hashed Weisfeiler–Lehman, cycle, Laplacian-spectral, and connectivity blocks — with invariance to node relabeling proven and confirmed numerically to $10^{-8}$ under random permutations. Isomorphic graphs map to identical inputs, removing node-order leakage by construction.
  2. A cross-verified, query-counted warm-start benchmark.Each policy proposes initial angles into a budgeted coordinate-ascent refiner, with every objective query charged against a fixed budget. The depth-one objective is computed by an analytic per-edge formula certified against an exact statevector simulator to $10^{-9}$, and approximation ratios are exact, measured against a brute-force MaxCut oracle rather than a relaxation bound.
  3. Family-held-out transfer under enforced leakage control.Policies are fit on one subset of graph families and evaluated on a held-out family, with a fingerprint-based programmatic check confirming that every test graph is absent from training (leakage_clean: true), rendering the transfer claim well posed by construction.
  4. A controlled null result, plus a query-efficiency edge.The learned topology-conditioned warm start matches rather than exceeds spectral initialization (both 0.7846 ± 0.0093); separately, every structure-aware policy reaches near-final quality on its first objective evaluation — the conservatively stated benefit that survives the comparison.

Methodological Novelty

The novelty resides in the experimental controls rather than in a new model: a leakage-aware, query-counted, cross-verified head-to-head against a strong-but-simple spectral baseline, underwritten by provable invariance.

Relative to the warm-start / angle-transfer literature

Concentration (Brandão et al.), parameter transfer (Galda et al., Shaydulin et al.), annealing schedules (Sack–Serbyn), warm-start rounding (Egger et al.), and meta-learning (Verdon et al., Khairy et al.) each contribute initializers, yet seldom under matched query budgets with leakage-controlled, family-held-out transfer measured against a spectral rule. This benchmark imposes precisely those controls, and under them the learned gain over spectral is statistically null on the final ratio.

Relative to graph learning for combinatorial optimization

Weisfeiler–Lehman kernels and message-passing GNNs (Xu et al., Gilmer et al., Veličković et al.; Dai et al., Cappart et al.) motivate graph-conditioned policies but do not, on their own, guarantee the invariance such a policy needs. Here the descriptor is a proven relabeling-invariant, dependency-light surrogate for a message-passing GNN — its WL refinement formally upper-bounds message-passing expressivity — and a trained GNN backend drops into the same slot without altering the harness.

Relative to depth-one QAOA analysis

The Wang et al. per-edge closed form serves here not just as a cheap angle evaluator but as one of two independent evaluators: it is derived analytically and then certified equal to the matrix-free statevector simulator (to $10^{-9}$). The fast $O(|E|)$ objective is therefore the exact circuit expectation, not an approximation of it.

Reporting discipline

Every reported number is regenerated by the accompanying code from fixed seeds and written to a single results/summary.json, and an audit script confirms that tables and figures trace back to those artifacts and that no unsupported superiority phrasing appears. The null result stands as written, with the $0.95$-target miss and the synthetic-graph scope kept in view.

Theoretical Guarantees

Each result is transcribed verbatim from the paper's Theory section, and each one licenses a specific experimental claim made elsewhere on this page.

Lemma 1 · ℤ₂ reduction and oracle correctness

$C(-z)=C(z)$ for all $z\in\{+1,-1\}^n$; fixing $z_0=+1$ and enumerating the $2^{n-1}$ remaining configurations visits every cut exactly once up to global flip, and the maximum equals the true MaxCut $C^\star$.

Guarantees that every approximation ratio $\langle C\rangle/C^\star$ is measured against exact ground truth, not a relaxation bound.
Lemma 2 · Factorization of the mixer unitary

$e^{-\mathrm{i}\beta B}=\bigotimes_{v=1}^{n}\bigl(\cos\beta\,\mathbb{I}-\mathrm{i}\sin\beta\,X\bigr)$, so the mixer acts by $n$ independent $2\times2$ operations and is never assembled as a $2^n\times2^n$ matrix.

Reduces the reference statevector evaluator to $O(p\,n\,2^n)$ from $O(2^{2n})$.
Proposition 3 · Depth-one per-edge closed form

$\langle C_{uv}\rangle=\tfrac12+\tfrac14\sin(4\beta)\sin\gamma\,[\cos^{d_u}\!\gamma+\cos^{d_v}\!\gamma]-\tfrac14\sin^2(2\beta)\cos^{\,d_u+d_v-2f}\!\gamma\,[1-\cos^{f}(2\gamma)]$, with $\langle C\rangle=\sum_{(u,v)\in E}\langle C_{uv}\rangle$.

Supplies the $O(|E|)$ objective used throughout — a depth-one specialization of the light-cone argument.
Corollary 4 · Closed-form / statevector equivalence

For every $(\gamma,\beta)$ and every $G$, the per-edge closed form summed over $E$ equals the statevector expectation $\langle\gamma,\beta|C|\gamma,\beta\rangle$; the two are the same real-analytic function and any discrepancy is floating-point only (verified to $10^{-9}$).

Licenses substituting the fast closed form for the simulator everywhere, with no loss of exactness.
Theorem 6 · Relabeling invariance of the descriptor map

For every permutation $\pi\in S_n$, $\phi(\pi G)=\phi(G)$ componentwise. In particular the learned regressor receives identical inputs for isomorphic graphs and cannot exploit node-order leakage.

Makes family-held-out transfer well posed and forecloses node-order leakage; verified numerically to $10^{-8}$.
Proposition 7 · WL equivariance and message-passing upper bound

The Weisfeiler–Lehman colour multiset after any fixed number of rounds is permutation invariant, and any message-passing GNN with permutation-invariant readout on the same initial features cannot distinguish two graphs that WL fails to distinguish.

Grounds the WL histogram as a principled, dependency-light stand-in for a GNN.
Corollary 8 · The learned policy is a deterministic class function

The graph-conditioned policy $\Theta(G)=\tfrac1k\sum_{j\in N_k(G)}\theta_j$ satisfies $\Theta(\pi G)=\Theta(G)$ and is a deterministic function of $G$ given the seeded training set.

Establishes the warm start as a principled estimator on isomorphism classes, reproducible across runs.
Proposition 9 · Monotonicity and warm-start floor of the refiner

The running-best ratio $r_t=\max(r_{t-1},\rho_t)$ is monotone non-decreasing in $t$ and satisfies $r_t\ge r_0$ for all $t$; a policy with high $r_0$ cannot be overtaken below $r_0$.

Explains the frontier's shape — the refiner equalizes structure-aware head starts onto a common ratio near 0.7846, with none collapsing below its starting value.

Significance and Impact

The work delivers a reusable yardstick for warm-start research, a sharpened open question, and an actionable conclusion for practitioners — with the study's limitations kept in plain view.

For the field. The five components — a proven relabeling-invariant descriptor, a query-counted refiner, two cross-verified objective evaluators, exact approximation ratios, and family-held-out splits with leakage checks — together constitute a benchmark against which future and genuinely stronger warm-start policies can be measured on equal terms. The controlled null result carries information of its own: as a disciplined extension of the angle-transfer literature, it shows that good depth-one angles are largely determined by coarse graph structure — structure that the one-line spectral rule and the learned descriptor capture about equally well.

For practice. The efficiency finding is the one that bears weight where it counts: structure-aware policies reach near-final quality from the very first query, precisely the regime in which each query is an expensive circuit execution. The benchmark turns this into a concrete open question — whether any learned policy can carry that early-budget advantage through refinement rather than having it erased — and ships the harness to answer it, including a drop-in slot for a trained message-passing GNN backend.

Honest limitations (carried from the paper, unmodified).
  • The study is confined to depth one, where the exact closed form keeps the objective cheap and smooth. Deeper circuits give rougher landscapes that may leave a learned policy more room, and there the closed form no longer holds.
  • Graphs are small ($n\le16$) so that exact MaxCut ground truth remains tractable; behaviour at advantage-relevant sizes is untested.
  • Graphs are synthetic, and the reported run uses a single master seed, so the confidence intervals cover the 120 graphs within that run, not independent dataset redraws.
  • Objective values are noiseless classical evaluations, free of shot or device noise; no quantum-hardware or superiority claim is made. The learned backend is a dependency-light descriptor regressor standing in for a GNN.

Reproducibility

The pipeline is CPU-only with no quantum-software dependency, and every number is regenerated from fixed seeds into a single source of truth, results/summary.json.

The complete reference implementation accompanies the manuscript in submission/code: graph generators, the relabeling-invariant descriptors, the analytic and statevector QAOA evaluators, the budgeted refiner, all five warm-start policies, family-held-out splitting and leakage checks, metrics, the experiment runner, the figure and table generators, and the test suite — including the closed-form-versus-statevector and descriptor-invariance checks. It depends only on NumPy, SciPy, NetworkX, scikit-learn, Matplotlib, and PyYAML, and installs as the package topoqaoa (v0.1.0, MIT).

A single master seed (0) deterministically fixes graph generation, the random-policy draws, the grid search that labels training graphs, and the cross-entropy sampler, and the leakage check passes on every fold (leakage_clean: true).
