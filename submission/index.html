Spectral-truncation graph kernels for QAOA warm starts: topology-conditioned schedule transfer beyond depth one

Molena Huynh

North Carolina State University, Raleigh, North Carolina 27695, USA · molena.huynh@jmp.com

GitHub repository: thmolena/Topological-Graph-for-QAOA

Abstract

Training a variational quantum circuit is stochastic optimization over an objective a quantum device only ever samples, so the scarce resource is queries to that estimator and the effective lever is a good statistical prior over where the optimum lies. We build such a prior as a kernel over graphs: the spectral-truncation kernel (STK), a relabeling-invariant, positive-definite graph kernel that transfers an optimized QAOA angle schedule from the most similar training graph, and use it to overturn the prevailing depth-one verdict that learned warm starts only match physics-based initializers. STK represents each graph by a finite low-frequency window of its normalized-Laplacian spectrum and donates the kernel-nearest graph's depth-p schedule. On a query-counted MaxCut benchmark over 84 connected graphs from six families under family-held-out splits, STK reproduces the depth-one parity (paired advantage +0.0000 ± 0.0001) and then beats the strongest adiabatic ramp by a margin that grows monotonically with depth, from +0.0103 at p = 2 to +0.0262 at p = 3, and by a wider one-shot margin still (+0.0416 at p = 2, +0.0454 at p = 3), since the transferred schedule is near-optimal on the first query. The kernel's invariance and positive definiteness are proved, kernel schedule regression obeys a representer theorem, a truncation-error bound, and a generalization bound with linear-in-depth sample complexity, the depth-one objective is cross-verified by two independent evaluators, and the pip-installable package specops-stk regenerates every figure, table, and number from fixed seeds.

Contributions

  1. Statistical theory. Proofs that the truncated spectrum and the full topology descriptor are relabeling invariant (Corollary 6, Theorem 7) and that STK is positive definite, making the warm start a deterministic function of the isomorphism class (Proposition 9, Corollary 10); a representer theorem for kernel schedule regression (Theorem 13), a rank-r truncation-error bound (Proposition 14), a Rademacher generalization bound with sample complexity linear in depth (Theorem 15, Corollary 16), and a beyond-depth-one Lipschitz transfer guarantee (Theorem 18). Born-rule sampling makes every landscape evaluation a statistical estimate (Lemma 12); the kernel surrogate is the interpretable statistical response.
  2. Algorithm. The STK warm-start operator: a product of Gaussian kernels on the truncated normalized-Laplacian spectrum and an invariant topology descriptor selects the single most similar solved instance and transfers its optimized 2p-angle schedule. Implemented with all baselines and exact evaluators in the pip package specops-stk (console command stk-reproduce).
  3. Empirical finding. The depth-one “learned warm starts only match” verdict is an artifact of p = 1. Topology-conditioned warm starts tie the ramp at p = 1 (+0.0000 ± 0.0001) and beat it at p = 2 (+0.0103 ± 0.0019 final, +0.0416 one-shot) and p = 3 (+0.0262 ± 0.0026 final, +0.0454 one-shot). STK attains the best one-shot ratio of any policy through p = 2; at p = 3 the averaging descriptor-mean learner draws statistically level with transfer (0.8925 vs 0.8902 final, overlapping 95% CIs).
  4. Benchmark. A reusable, controlled yardstick: exact approximation ratios against a brute-force MaxCut oracle, a depth-one objective cross-verified to 10−9 by two independent evaluators, family-held-out splits with a programmatic leakage check, and query-counted one-shot and budget-frontier views.

Reproduce every result

Every figure, table, and number in the manuscript is regenerated deterministically (fixed seed 0) by the package; nothing is typed by hand.

pip install specops-stk      # or: pip install ./submission/code
stk-reproduce                # full reported scale, about 7 minutes on a laptop CPU
stk-reproduce --quick        # smoke-scale end-to-end check, a few seconds
stk-reproduce --skip-run     # replot all figures/tables from the shipped summary.json

The run writes results/summary.json (with seed, platform, versions, runtime, and peak-memory provenance), regenerates the four manuscript figures (fig_schematic, fig_depth, fig_frontier, fig_family) and all tables/macros, and mirrors them into the manuscript tree. The test suite (make test) asserts the closed-form/statevector cross-check to 10−9, descriptor and kernel invariance to 10−8, and kernel positive semidefiniteness. See code/README.md for the figure-by-figure mapping to generating functions.
